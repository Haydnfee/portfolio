60s Style Research

Instead of talking about how this is made, let's look at the actual style and what makes this look like a piece of "60s pop culture".

First let's look at the typography. Around the late 50s, typography would take a more flourished turn. Instead of the standard arial and monospaced fonts, bold flowing and "handwritten" fonts started popping up paired with bold color pallettes. Alot of different franchises are going back and usingthese old designs as it looks retro or as companies such as Coke describe it, "classic".

Speaking of colors, what influence my pallette choice? It's actually simple. Think of a setting that movies set in the 60s would use. Schools, military bases, and diners to name a few. One of these stands out. Diners. They have a unique style that is recognizable, but isnt used in modern day locations. I found some images of cafes from around that era and current day nostalgia cafes. Bright faded reds, bold teals and greens, and black and white tiling are dominant throughout most of the examples.
